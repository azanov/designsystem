Lean UX

We apply Lean UX principles to the way we think, act, collaborate and design our products to be loved by our clients and users.

“Life’s too short to build something nobody wants.”— Ash Maurya

Overview

Lean UX is the practice of bringing the true nature of product to light faster, in a collaborative and cross-functional way. It does this by focusing on building a shared understanding of the product experience that’s being designed.

In order for this to happen, three things must come together:

  • Design thinking
  • Lean principles
  • Agile delivery

Design Thinking

  • Focuses on discovering customer needs
  • Validates that the problems we’re trying to solve exist
  • Ensures that the solutions we produce are a market fit

“Design thinking is a human-centered approach to innovation that draws from the designer’s toolkit to integrate the needs of people, the possibilities of technology, and the requirements for business success.”
— Tim Brown, president and CEO, Ideo

Startup Principles

  • Defined by the “Build, Measure & Learn&rdquo cycle, with critical emphasis on learning
  • Promotes an experimental mindset of building minimal viable products (MVP)
  • Make measurement against clearly defined Key Performance Indicators (KPI)
  • Uses learning to deliver better product in every iteration

Agile Delivery

  • Focuses development on creating immediate value
  • Delivers working software to customers quickly
  • Iterates the product according to new learning

Methods

Multi-track Development Process

Continuous Delivery Track

  • Starts with an early discovery phase to define the product challenge and create a shared client understanding
  • Includes a short 4-6 week cycle of cross-functional teamwork to elaborate upon each prioritized epic
  • Validates conceptual wireframes and prototypes with clients, users and stakeholders

Detailed Design Track

  • Designers add and elaborate upon details to create conceptual prototypes needed for development
  • Designs are delivered in a consumable format (HTML), which reduces the need for heavily documented handoffs

Agile Delivery Track

  • Dedicated designers and UX developers participate in daily scrum activities
  • UX developers integrate their front-end pieces with business logic and service layers

Product Assessment

We ask some fundamental questions before proceeding with design and development:

  1. Exactly what problem will this solve? (value proposition)
  2. For whom do we solve that problem? (target market)
  3. How big is the opportunity? (market size)
  4. What alternatives are out there? (competitive landscape)
  5. Why are we best suited to pursue this? (our differentiator)
  6. Why now? (market window)
  7. How will we get this product to market? (go-to-market strategy)
  8. How will we measure success/make money from this product? (metrics/revenue strategy)
  9. What factors are critical to success? (solution requirements)
  10. Given the above, what’s the recommendation? (go or no-go)
