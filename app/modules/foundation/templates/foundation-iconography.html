Icon Strategy

We’ve created a family of icons and illustrations with an expanding hierarchy that fans out with more choices as the required use broadens.

When using icons, considerations should be made for what type of page the illustration or icon will be used on (for example, marketing page vs. product app) and what size the mark will be applied. Every icon is created to feel as if from the same family, no matter where it is used. We use illustrations at larger sizes and icons at smaller sizes when less detail/style can be conveyed.

Category illustrations

Our category illustrations sit just below our brand’s logo in terms of their specific use, as they are tied to our five business units. They are mainly used on marketing materials and landing pages. They are illustrations rather than icons because of their complexity and lack of scalability, along with particular application requirements.

Brand Illustrations

Brand illustrations symbolize category and concept names used for any landing page, marketing material or application within any of our five business units.

Brand Icons

Brand icons are simplified versions of the brand illustrations that can be used at smaller scales.

Functional Icons

Functional icons are used to quickly convey a command, a category, or common actions or labels within an application. They are used as live navigation/commands within the UI.

Brand Icons
