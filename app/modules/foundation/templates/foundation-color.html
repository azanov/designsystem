Color Palettes

Our primary color palette is designed to display well, with optimum contrast, on the web and on mobile devices. Our foundational brand colors — cyan and magenta — don’t read well in small type sizes on digital screens. So they’ve been limited to use as accent colors and large type sizes only. Our auxiliary color palette contains colors that should be used sparingly due to their potential to clash with our other two palettes.

Application

Choose three colors — one primary color, one accent color, and one auxiliary color — and then use only those colors (and its variations in saturation) throughout your product. Since PB Magenta pairs well with all colors, we highly recommended that you use it as your secondary color.

Neutral Palettes

Our neutral palette’s colors are used across elements that make up the underlying framework of your product, such as typography and containers. Darker colors are primarily used for type. Lighter colors are exclusively used for backgrounds, hovers and containers.

Gradients

Our gradients are based on our primary and accent palettes and can be used to add visual interest to your product’s navigation. A gradient always consists of a combination of a primary color on the left and an accent color on the right. While this system allows for a range of options we recommend using the first gradient shown (PB Blue transitioning to PB Magenta), to keep our products looking consistent.
